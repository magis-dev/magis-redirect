Safety Standards & Reporting

Magis is committed to preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on our platform. Below are our published safety standards and how to report suspected CSAE.

Published Safety Standards (in full)

Magis maintains the following safety standards to prevent, detect, and respond to child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE):

  1. Zero-tolerance policy: Any content or behavior that sexualizes or exploits minors is strictly prohibited. Accounts, profiles, and content that violate this are removed immediately.
  2. Proactive moderation: We use a mix of automated detection (hash-matching, image/video analysis, metadata checks) and human review to identify potential CSAE content and remove it rapidly.
  3. User reporting: We provide clear in-app reporting and this reporting contact for users to report suspected CSAE. Reports are triaged within 24 hours and escalated as needed.
  4. Preservation & escalation: When CSAE is suspected, we preserve evidence (logs, media, metadata) and, where required by law, promptly notify and cooperate with law enforcement and child protection authorities.
  5. Account actions: When an account is found to be involved in CSAE, we will suspend or terminate the account and remove related content. Repeat or severe violations result in permanent bans.
  6. Data minimization & privacy: We collect only the information necessary to operate the service and investigations. We store investigative records securely and limit access to authorized personnel.
  7. Transparency & training: Moderation staff receive regular training on CSAE recognition, evidence handling, and safe reporting. We maintain internal processes for timely takedowns and disclosure to authorities.
  8. Third-party cooperation: We cooperate with industry hotlines, NGOs, and law enforcement for rapid reporting and takedown of CSAE material.
  9. Timelines: Takedown actions for verified CSAE are performed immediately upon confirmation; initial triage occurs within 24 hours of a report.
